The entryway is the smallest room in your home — and the most-photographed. It sets the temperature for everything else. Here's how to make yours feel calm and curated, even if it's barely a hallway.

## Start with one piece of sculptural furniture

A travertine or oak side table is enough. Just one surface to drop keys, a candle, a small dish for change. Resist the urge to add a console *and* a bench *and* a shoe rack.

## Hang a mirror — wavy or arched

A mirror does triple duty: it bounces light, makes the space feel larger, and gives you one last glance before you walk out the door. A wavy or arched silhouette feels modern without trying too hard.

## Add a single, low-maintenance plant

Either a real one you actually want to keep alive (a ZZ plant, a pothos) or a high-quality faux olive. Skip the small succulents — they read as "office desk."

## Soften the floor

If your entryway has hard flooring, drop a small, low-pile runner in a natural fiber. The texture cues "slow down" the moment someone steps inside.

## Curate the wall

One piece of art — oversized if possible. Resist the gallery wall here. The entryway is the room where one confident piece does more than twelve tiny ones.

## Light it warmly

If you can, swap your entry bulb for a warm-white, dimmable LED. The color temperature alone will make the space feel intentional.

> A great entryway says: *someone thoughtful lives here*. It doesn't have to be expensive — it has to be edited.
